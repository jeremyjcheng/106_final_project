Have you ever wondered how factories influence rainfall?

As you scroll, factories begin to rise along the horizon. Each new facility represents the growth of human activity, while the clouds above still look thick, bright, and almost untouched.

Keep scrolling to follow the smoke ⬇

Factories release streams of SO₄-based aerosols into the sky.

Microscopic sulfate particles rise from smokestacks and drift toward the cloud layer. They are not water vapor themselves, yet they quietly reshape how clouds form, grow, and fade.

Excess aerosols can thin the clouds and make rain less likely.

As SO₄ particles accumulate, cloud droplets struggle to merge into raindrops. The clouds become patchy and faint, and once condensation weakens, rainfall starts to decline as well.
